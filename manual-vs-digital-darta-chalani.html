Manual vs Digital Darta Chalani System in Nepal

Published by Basant Balayar | Digital Governance Nepal

The Darta Chalani System is a core administrative process in Nepalese government offices. For decades, offices relied on manual paper-based registers. However, with the rise of digital governance, many institutions are transitioning to digital darta chalani systems.

What is Manual Darta Chalani?

Manual Darta Chalani is a traditional system where incoming and outgoing documents are recorded in physical registers. Each document entry is written by hand, making the process slow and dependent on human accuracy.

Problems with Manual Darta Chalani

  • Slow document registration process
  • Difficulty in searching old records
  • Risk of file loss or damage
  • No real-time tracking
  • Audit preparation is time-consuming

What is Digital Darta Chalani?

Digital Darta Chalani is a software-based solution that electronically records incoming and outgoing documents. It provides centralized access, fast search, tracking, and reporting features.

Benefits of Digital Darta Chalani System

  • Instant document registration and retrieval
  • Secure data storage with backups
  • Improved transparency and accountability
  • Audit-ready reports
  • Supports paperless governance
